FIGURE 1) PERMUTATION-BASED NULL COMPARISON
------------------------------------------------------------

Why
- This is the cleanest statistical validation.
- It tests whether the observed M-Reg structure disappears when the pairing between M and Reg is broken.

What to do
- Keep the observed methylation array fixed.
- Shuffle Reg across bins.
- Recompute the same quantile-based observed/expected heatmap.
- Compare observed, shuffled, and observed-minus-shuffled.

Best output
- One 3-column panel per mark:
  - observed O/E heatmap
  - shuffled O/E heatmap
  - observed minus shuffled heatmap
- Also write a TSV with observed and shuffled Spearman and mutual information.

FIGURE 2) MUTUAL INFORMATION ACROSS RESOLUTIONS AND MARKS
------------------------------------------------------------

Why
- Mutual information is a compact non-linear dependence summary.
- It complements Pearson and Spearman and lets you compare resolutions directly.

What to do
- For each mark and resolution, bin M and Reg into quantiles.
- Compute MI on the 2D contingency table.
- Optionally subtract the mean shuffled-null MI.

Best output
- Grouped bar plot or heatmap:
  - x-axis = resolution
  - groups = marks
  - y-axis = MI or delta-MI over null

Interpretation guidance
- If the shuffled null removes the structure, that is the strongest validation.
- If MI is largest at broader resolution, that supports a scale-dependent dependence claim.
- If violin or box plots show that high Reg becomes rare at high M, that is biologically stronger than a mean-only trend.
- If promoters or CGI promoters show stronger structure than background, that supports biological specificity.
- If cluster overlap is partial rather than perfect, that supports related-but-not-redundant information content.
